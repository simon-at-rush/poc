The Challenge: Growth Without Depth

Vietnam's tertiary education has seen explosive enrollment growth, driven by a rising middle class. However, this rapid expansion has outpaced the development of quality and resources, creating a system under immense strain. This section explores the key dynamics of enrollment, student mobility, and the most popular fields of study.

A critical failure of the system is the persistent mismatch between university training and labor market needs. This results in underemployed graduates and skill shortages in key economic sectors. The Vision 2045: A "Leapfrog" Strategy

The Vietnamese government has launched an ambitious, top-down reform agenda to modernize its education system. The strategy aims to achieve global standards by 2045 through aggressive internationalization, massive R&D investment, and granting universities greater autonomy. The Future: Disruptive Trends

While systemic challenges persist, powerful trends are reshaping the education landscape from the outside in. The rise of private universities, the EdTech revolution, and deeper global partnerships are introducing new models, capital, and competition that will define the sector's future trajectory.
