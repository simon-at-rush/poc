Pathways to Persistence: Strategic Solutions

Achieving the sub-10% target requires a coordinated, multifaceted strategy that addresses the root causes of the problem.

  • Strengthen Career Guidance: Develop professional, comprehensive career counseling programs in high schools to help students choose majors that fit their interests and abilities.
  • Diversify Admissions Methods: Encourage universities to adopt more holistic admissions models, not just relying on test scores, to ensure a better fit between students and programs.
  • Elevate Vocational Education (VET): Position VET as a valuable choice, not a backup plan, to reduce the pressure to enter university at all costs.
  • Mandate "First-Year Experience" Programs: Design first-year curricula focused on essential study skills, early major exposure, and proactive academic advising.
  • Invest in Student Support Services: Expand access to academic advising, mental health counseling, and financial aid support to help students overcome challenges.
  • Link Funding to Outcomes: Reform budget allocation to tie a portion of funding to success metrics like retention, timely graduation, and graduate employment rates.
  • Targeted Financial Aid: Expand scholarship and grant programs for students from disadvantaged backgrounds, especially from rural and ethnic minority areas.
  • Close the Urban-Rural Gap: Strategically invest to improve the quality of K-12 education in remote regions to enhance students' academic preparedness.
  • Strengthen Industry-University Linkages: Create policies to encourage collaboration on curriculum development and internships, ensuring student skills meet market demands.
