Pathways to Change

Despite the challenges, promising approaches and powerful levers for change are emerging. These "bright spots" move away from a deficit model towards one that values self-determination, cultural safety, and holistic wellbeing. Hover over or tap the cards below to learn more about these transformative solutions.

Tino Rangatiratanga

The Whānau Ora Model

An Indigenous-led, strengths-based approach placing whānau aspirations at the centre. It shifts power and resources to communities, proving that self-determination is a powerful public health intervention.

Pacific-Led Solutions

Fonofale & Yavu Frameworks

Grounded in culturally resonant models of health like Fonofale, these approaches deliver holistic, on-the-ground support through trusted community organisations, ensuring services are meaningful and effective for Pacific peoples.

Cultural Safety

Transforming the Clinical Encounter

Moves beyond 'competence' to require providers to reflect on their own biases and the power dynamics of care. It aims to create a safe, respectful environment where the patient is empowered.

Systemic Levers

Governance, Funding, Workforce, Data

Targeting high-leverage points like embedding Te Tiriti in governance, reforming funding models to reward wellbeing, building a representative workforce, and establishing data sovereignty can create large-scale systemic change.

Rural & Digital Health

Bridging the Urban Divide

A dedicated Rural Health Strategy and the use of telehealth can improve access. This requires integrated care models and investment to close the "digital divide" to ensure technology reduces, not widens, inequity.

The 2022 Reforms

A Structural Shift

The creation of Te Aka Whai Ora | Māori Health Authority represented a bold structural embodiment of Te Tiriti principles. Its disestablishment highlights the vulnerability of reforms to political ideology.

A Global View

Aotearoa's struggle for health equity is not unique. Examining other nations offers valuable lessons. Here, we compare our system with other settler-colonial nations facing similar challenges and with the Nordic countries, which offer a different model built on public equity.

The Staggering Cost of Inaction

Health inequity is not only a moral failure but a massive economic burden. The current system creates perverse incentives, "saving" money on upstream care while communities bear the devastating cost of premature death and lost potential.
